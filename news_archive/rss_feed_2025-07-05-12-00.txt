Additionally, Hpingbot malware is targeting SSH services to launch distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks, highlighting ongoing threats to server infrastructure.

A previously undocumented advanced persistent threat (APT) group named NightEagle (APT-Q-95) has been identified exploiting zero-day vulnerabilities in Microsoft Exchange servers. Their targets include government, defense, and technology sectors in China. Active since 2023, NightEagle employs sophisticated exploit chains and has adapted its network infrastructure to evade detection.

4. **Risks of Data Leakage from AI Agents and Generative AI Workflows**

5. **Critical Sudo Vulnerabilities Allow Local Privilege Escalation on Linux**  
Two security flaws in the widely used Sudo utility for Linux and Unix-like systems have been disclosed. These vulnerabilities allow local attackers to escalate privileges to root, potentially compromising major Linux distributions. One notable flaw (CVE-2025-32462) affects Sudo versions prior to 1.9.17p1 under certain sudoers configurations.
